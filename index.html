Ad & Tracker Blocking
Blocks ads, trackers, crypto miners & data brokers at the network level
Active
Community-maintained filter lists — 100,000+ rules covering ad domains, pixel trackers, and social widgets. Lists are cached locally and updated in the background. Every request on every tab is checked.
URL parameter stripping — UTM codes, fbclid, gclid, mc_eid, ttclid, twclid, msclkid and 50+ other tracking tokens are removed from every URL before it loads. Sites can't correlate your clicks across campaigns or platforms.
Third-party cookie isolation — cross-site cookies are blocked so ad networks can't build a behavioural profile across different sites. Facebook Pixel, Google Analytics, and similar trackers see nothing.
Crypto miner & data broker blocking — a curated list targets sites that mine cryptocurrency using your CPU or sell your personal data to third parties.
CNAME uncloaking — some trackers disguise themselves behind first-party DNS aliases. Lander resolves CNAME records and blocks the underlying tracker domain even when it's hidden behind a first-party subdomain.
Fingerprint Defence
Randomises your digital identity every session
Active
Canvas & WebGL noise — invisible, session-unique noise is injected into every canvas and WebGL readback. Each fingerprint attempt returns different, useless data. Your visual identity changes every session.
Spoofed user-agent — Lander presents itself as standard Chrome on Windows to websites. Sites that block alternative browsers let you through. The real Electron runtime is never exposed.
WebRTC IP leak protection — WebRTC is configured to only expose your public IP. Local network addresses, VPN adapter IPs, and ISP-assigned internal addresses are never leaked to web pages.
Audio context noise — AudioContext fingerprinting is defeated by injecting session-unique noise, preventing ad networks from identifying you through your audio hardware characteristics.
Hardware concurrency & device memory — Lander reports generic values for navigator.hardwareConcurrency and navigator.deviceMemory, preventing sites from narrowing your identity using system specs.
Zero Telemetry
No data collection, no servers, no cloud sync, no analytics
Always
Everything stored locally — history, bookmarks, passwords, notes, and settings live in local files on your machine. There is no Lander Browser server. No cloud sync. No upload. Nothing leaves your device.
Engine phone-home disabled — Safe Browsing reports, crash dumps, translation pings, spelling service, metrics reporting, and component update checks are all disabled. All Chromium-level data collection is silenced.
No background networking — Lander passes --disable-background-networking at launch, preventing Chromium from making undocumented network requests when you're not actively browsing.
No analytics or tracking SDK — Lander does not include Google Analytics, Sentry, Mixpanel, or any other analytics framework. There is literally no code in the browser that could phone home.
Network Hardening
DNS, referrer, and request-level protections
Active
Referrer policy — Lander sends a strict referrer policy, preventing sites from seeing the full URL you came from. Only the origin is shared, not the full path or query parameters.
Secure DNS — DNS-over-HTTPS is enabled, encrypting your DNS queries so your ISP cannot see which domains you visit.
Permission defaults — Camera, microphone, geolocation, notifications, and other sensitive permissions default to "ask" or "block". No site gets automatic access to your hardware.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026 · Effective upon first use

1. Our Core Commitment

Lander Browser is built on a single principle: your browsing is your business. We do not operate servers that receive your browsing data. We do not sell, share, or monetise your personal information — ever. Everything stays on your device.

2. What We Never Collect

The following data is never collected, transmitted, or stored by Lander Browser on any server we operate:

  • Your browsing history or page visit logs
  • Search queries you enter
  • URLs or domains you navigate to
  • Your IP address or approximate geolocation
  • Cookies, login sessions, or authentication tokens
  • Passwords or form data
  • Screenshots or page content
  • Bookmarks or saved notes
  • Any personally identifiable information (PII)

3. Local Storage

All user data — including bookmarks, passwords, browsing history, saved notes, settings, and session data — is stored exclusively on your local device. This data is never uploaded, synced, or shared with any external server or cloud service. You can delete it at any time via Settings → Privacy → Clear Data.

4. Data Privacy & Collection

Lander Browser is designed with privacy as a core principle. The application does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data, browsing history, search terms, or usage statistics. All browsing data remains locally on your device. The application includes no telemetry, analytics, or tracking mechanisms. Any optional features that may involve data processing (such as translation services) use third-party services with their own privacy policies.

5. Search Engines & Third-Party Services

When you perform a search, your query is sent directly to your chosen search engine (e.g. Google, DuckDuckGo). Lander Browser does not intercept, log, proxy, or modify these requests. Your search queries are governed entirely by your search engine's own privacy policy.

6. Ad Blocking & Filter Lists

Lander Browser downloads publicly available filter lists (EasyList, EasyPrivacy, EasyAnnoyances) anonymously from their official CDN endpoints. These lists are cached locally. Filter-list CDN providers may log your IP address as part of standard access logging — this is outside our control.

7. Automatic Update Checks

When you use "Check for Updates", Lander Browser contacts the GitHub Releases API. This request may include your IP address as a standard HTTP header. No additional user data is transmitted. GitHub's privacy policy applies.

8. yt-dlp & Video Downloads

When you use the video downloader, yt-dlp makes direct HTTP requests to the target website from your device. Lander Browser does not proxy or log these requests. The target platform will see your IP address. These interactions are governed by the respective platform's privacy policies.

9. Discord Rich Presence (Optional)

If Discord is running, Lander Browser may display a "Rich Presence" status via a local socket connection. No browsing data or URLs are shared with Discord. Discord's own privacy policy governs what Discord does with Rich Presence data. You can disable this feature at any time.

10. Connectivity Checks

The offline-detection feature makes an anonymous DNS-over-HTTPS request to dns.google to verify internet connectivity. No user data is attached to this request. Google's privacy policy applies.

11. Children's Privacy

Lander Browser does not knowingly collect any personal information from users under the age of 13 (or the applicable age of digital consent in your jurisdiction). If you believe a child has provided personal data, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

12. Your Rights

Because Lander Browser stores all data locally and does not transmit it to us, you exercise your data rights directly on your device. You can view, export, and delete all locally stored data at any time via Settings.

13. Data Security

We design Lander Browser to minimise data exposure. We strongly recommend enabling full-disk encryption on your operating system, keeping Lander Browser updated, and using a strong system login password to protect locally stored data.

14. Third-Party Data Controllers

Certain optional features of Lander Browser make use of third-party services that may independently process your IP address or other network metadata. These third parties act as independent data controllers under their own privacy policies:

  • Google Public DNS (dns.google) — Used for anonymous offline-detection connectivity checks.
  • GitHub (api.github.com) — Used for version update checks and filter list hosting.
  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com) — Used to serve the Geist UI font. May receive your IP as part of the CDN request.
  • EasyList CDN (easylist-downloads.adblockplus.org) — Used to update ad and tracking filter lists. Standard access logs may include your IP.
  • Widevine licence servers — Contacted only if you play DRM-protected media. Governed by Google LLC's privacy policy.

Lander Browser does not broker, monetise, or receive any data from these third-party controllers. Their processing is incidental to providing specific optional features.

15. Cookies & Browser Storage

Lander Browser stores cookies and local-storage data set by websites you visit. This data is kept exclusively on your device. Lander Browser does not read or transmit cookies to us. You can clear cookies at any time via Settings → Privacy → Clear Data. Third-party websites' use of cookies is governed by their own privacy policies.

16. Third-Party Website Data Practices

When you browse third-party websites using Lander Browser, those websites operate under their own independent privacy policies. Lander Browser has no knowledge of, control over, or responsibility for how third-party websites collect, store, process, sell, or transmit your personal data. Any data you submit to a third-party website — including usernames, passwords, payment details, personal information, or any other information — is governed solely by that website's privacy policy and applicable law. We strongly encourage you to read the privacy policy of every website you visit.

17. Third-Party Security & Data Breaches

Lander Browser cannot protect you from security vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, or malicious acts by third-party websites or services. If a website you visit using Lander Browser suffers a data breach, ransomware attack, insider threat, server compromise, or any other security incident resulting in the exposure, theft, or loss of your data, Lander Browser bears no responsibility whatsoever. The security posture, data-handling practices, and incident-response capabilities of third-party services are entirely outside our control and visibility.

18. No Warranty of Third-Party Site Security

Lander Browser is a navigation tool; it does not scan, audit, certify, or vouch for the security of any website you visit. You access third-party websites entirely at your own risk.

19. Credentials & Authentication Data

If login credentials stored in Lander Browser's local password manager are entered into a third-party website that is subsequently compromised, Lander Browser accepts no liability for the compromise of those credentials or any resulting harm. We recommend using unique, strong passwords for every website and enabling multi-factor authentication wherever possible. The use of a separate, dedicated password manager with additional encryption is also advisable.

20. Malicious or Deceptive Websites

Lander Browser includes optional safe-browsing and anti-phishing indicators, but these features do not guarantee detection of all malicious, fraudulent, or deceptive websites. If you visit a website that installs malware on your device, steals your credentials via phishing, executes drive-by exploits, or engages in any other harmful behaviour, Lander Browser is not liable for any resulting harm, data loss, financial loss, or device damage. Browsing the internet inherently carries risk. You are responsible for exercising caution about which websites you visit and what information you provide to them.

21. Aggregate Anonymised Telemetry

Lander Browser does not collect any telemetry. However, certain optional network requests (filter list updates, connectivity checks, update verification) may result in your IP address being visible to third-party infrastructure providers (GitHub, Google DNS, CDN operators). These providers operate under their own separate privacy policies, which we incorporate by reference. We are not a controller of that data.

22. Retention & Deletion of Local Data

Because all user data is stored locally, Lander Browser has no ability to remotely delete, recover, or restore your data. If your local storage is corrupted, deleted, or lost due to hardware failure, operating system error, accidental deletion, user action, or any other cause, we bear no liability for such loss. We strongly recommend that you maintain regular backups of your device and any important locally stored data.

Continued use after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy. If material changes are made, we will note the new "Last updated" date accordingly.
